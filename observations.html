In Depth Observations

I've been drawn to depth maps after seeing them for the first time. They show a different view of this world that provided for plenty of intellectual stimulation.
Before you read about my observations, why not first take a look for yourself and see what you observe?

Press the space bar or click the image to see the photo which the depth map has been derived from.

Here are some of my personal observations and thoguhts on the topic, mixed with philosophical ideas I was contemplating while working on this page:

  • Night/day differentiation is impossible.
  • Object surfaces: It's hard to know what a surface is made of — wood, concrete, metal — in the depth map they all look the same.
  • Writing, paintings, art: From the depth map you can't make out written text and paintings, you can't enjoy (2D) art, can't read signs, etc.
  • Color plays no role in a depth-only view of the world.
  • You can't recognize people from depth maps.
  • Objects in the far distance, like mountains in a panoramic image, can't be seen.
Philosophical
  • Analytic Idealism philosopher Bernardo Kastrup often mentions the dashboard of an airplane as a metaphor to explain the human experience of the world. Viewing the world through depth maps or depth map videos allowed me to change some of the dashboard instruments.
  • Bats use depth for navigation. What Is It Like to Be a Bat? became a little more tangible for me.
